Many of our accounts are online these days and it can be difficult to keep account information organized.

Choose the statement that best describes you.

I use the same password for most of my accounts.

This method can be very convenient, but is also very risky. Imagine the damage that could be done if the wrong person got their hands on your password.

I use the same 3-5 passwords for most of my accounts.

Using 3-5 passowrds is a great start but there is more you can do to keep your accounts secure.

I use a random unique password for each of my accounts.

Your password management skills are on point. Using a unique password for each account ensures that if one account is compromised, the others are still safe.

Quick Tips

Hackers are constantly trying to tap into our accounts by stealing passwords. If you use the same password for multiple accounts, then your personal data is vulnerable.

Effective password security is crucial to maintaining your data security. The single most effective step you can take to secure your accounts is to use a password manager.

A Password Manager is a tool that stores your usernames and passwords in one secure place. A good password manager will generate a unique password for each different account that you use, and then automatically sign you in when needed. As long as your password management account is secure, then so are your accounts.
